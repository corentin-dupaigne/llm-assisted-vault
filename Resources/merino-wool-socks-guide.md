## Material Science: The "Golden Ratio"

### Recommended Fabric Blend: Merino Wool Hybrid

- **Optimal Composition:** 60–70% Merino Wool / 25–35% Nylon / 2–5% Elastane (Spandex).

- **Rationale:** 100% natural wool lacks the structural integrity for daily wear and will develop holes quickly. The addition of Nylon provides abrasion resistance, while Elastane ensures the sock maintains its shape and stay-up power.

- **Avoid:** 100% Cotton (absorbs moisture and stays wet, leading to blisters and fungal growth) or 100% Polyester (traps heat and promotes bacterial odor).

## Physiological Benefits

- **Thermoregulation:** Merino wool has a natural "crimp" that creates microscopic air pockets, acting as a buffer against both heat and cold.

- **Antimicrobial Efficacy:** Unlike synthetic fibers, wool absorbs odor molecules into its core, releasing them only during washing.

- **Hydrophobic/Hydrophilic Balance:** Wool can absorb up to 30% of its weight in moisture without feeling damp, keeping the skin surface dry.

## Clinical Evidence & Research

1. **Thermal Comfort and Moisture Management:**

   - **Source:** _Laing, R.M., et al. "Thermoregulatory responses of the foot to different sock compositions." Textile Research Journal._

   - **Findings:** Wool-rich blends consistently outperform cotton and pure synthetics in maintaining stable skin temperature and reducing "post-exercise chill."

1. **Bacterial Growth and Odor Control:**

   - **Source:** _McQueen, R. H., et al. "Odor intensity of wear-trialed textiles." Journal of the Textile Institute._

   - **Findings:** Wool textiles were rated significantly lower for odor intensity than polyester after identical wear periods, due to the fiber's ability to trap volatile organic compounds (VOCs).

1. **Friction and Blister Prevention:**

   - **Source:** _Knapik, J. J., et al. "Socks for Blister Prevention." Journal of the American Podiatric Medical Association._

   - **Findings:** Moisture-wicking fibers (Merino/Nylon) reduce the coefficient of friction on the skin, significantly lowering the risk of shear-induced trauma (blisters).
