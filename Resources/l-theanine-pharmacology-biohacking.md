L-Theanine is a non-proteinogenic amino acid found primarily in tea leaves (_Camellia sinensis_) and certain species of fungi. It is a cornerstone of pharmacological biohacking due to its unique ability to promote relaxation and focus without inducing sedation.

______________________________________________________________________

## 1. Primary Mechanisms of Action

L-Theanine easily crosses the blood-brain barrier and influences neurochemistry through several distinct pathways:

- **Alpha Brain Wave Induction:** It significantly increases alpha-1 brain wave activity ($8\\text{--}13\\text{ Hz}$), which is associated with a state of "relaxed alertness" or "flow."
- **Glutamate Antagonism:** It acts as a weak antagonist to glutamate receptors (the brain's primary excitatory neurotransmitter), effectively "turning down the volume" on over-excitation and anxiety.
- **GABA and Dopamine Modulation:** It supports the production of GABA, dopamine, and serotonin, contributing to improved mood and anxiolytic (anti-anxiety) effects.
- **Caffeine Synergy:** It mitigates the vasoconstrictive effects of caffeine, which helps maintain blood flow to the brain and eliminates the "jittery" side effects of stimulants.

______________________________________________________________________

## 2. Documented Benefits

L-Theanine is one of the most clinically validated nootropics for cognitive and emotional regulation:

- **Clean Focus:** Enhances selective attention and task switching, especially when paired with stimulants.
- **Stress Reduction:** Lowers heart rate and salivary cortisol levels in response to acute stressors (e.g., high-pressure meetings or multitasking).
- **Sleep Quality Enhancement:** Unlike sedatives, it improves sleep _quality_ by lowering nighttime anxiety and resting heart rate rather than forcing unconsciousness.
- **Antidote to Over-Stimulation:** Effectively reduces the peripheral side effects of caffeine, such as tremors and increased blood pressure.

## 4. Administration and Dosage

L-Theanine has an exceptionally high safety ceiling, with clinical studies using doses up to $900\\text{ mg}$ without significant adverse effects.

|**Metric**|**Recommendation**|
|---|---|
|**Focus/Attention**|$100\\text{mg -- }250\\text{mg}$ (usually taken with caffeine).|
|**Anxiety/Stress Relief**|$200\\text{mg -- }400\\text{mg}$ per dose.|
|**Sleep Support**|$400\\text{mg -- }500\\text{mg}$ taken 30 minutes before bed.|
|**Half-Life**|Approximately $3\\text{--}5$ hours; effects are felt within 40 minutes.|

## 5. Potential Side Effects and Interactions

L-Theanine is "Generally Recognized as Safe" (GRAS) by the FDA.

- **Side Effects:** Extremely rare, but very high doses may cause mild headaches or lightheadedness in sensitive individuals.

- **Drug Interactions:**

  - **Antihypertensives:** Because L-Theanine can lower blood pressure, it may have an additive effect when taken with blood pressure medications.
  - **Stimulants:** It is often used to balance ADHD medications, but you should consult a physician before combining it with prescription stimulants.
